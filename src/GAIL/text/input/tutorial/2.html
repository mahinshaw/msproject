Tutorial Part II: What is an argument? How do I build a simple argument?

In this learning environment, a simple argument for a claim has three parts:

  • The hypothesis: the statement that is being argued for or against.
  • The data: information from the patient's medical record that is evidence for the hypothesis.
  • The connection: one or more pieces of general scientific or medical information that relates the data to the hypothesis. Note: you can drag more than one block of information from the Connections section of the screen and snap them together in the argument workspace

For example, suppose the problem is to build an argument for the hypothesis: "The cause of the patient's hearing loss is due to having two mutated alleles of the gene called GJB2." To watch this argument being constructed with the tools you have in this learning environment, play the video below.
